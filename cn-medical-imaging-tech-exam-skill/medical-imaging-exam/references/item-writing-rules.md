# Item Writing Rules

## General rules

- Generate original questions only.
- Do not copy, paraphrase, or imitate real exam questions.
- Use clear Chinese wording suitable for professional title exam preparation.
- Each item must have one and only one best answer.
- Options A-E should be parallel in grammar, length, and specificity.
- Distractors should be plausible and exam-relevant.
- Avoid obviously absurd distractors.
- Avoid answer clues caused by wording, length, absolutes, or overly specific details.
- Avoid ambiguous stems.
- Avoid overly broad or vague questions.

## Stem design

A good stem should:

- State a clear task.
- Focus on one knowledge point or one integrated practice decision.
- Provide all information needed to answer.
- Avoid unnecessary clinical background.
- Avoid trick questions.

## Explanation design

Each explanation should:

- Explain why the correct answer is best.
- Briefly explain why major distractors are not best.
- Stay within imaging technology exam scope.
- Avoid making unsupported clinical claims.

## Originality requirement

If reference materials include real or suspected real exam questions, do not rewrite them. Extract only high-level knowledge points and create a new scenario, new stem structure, new options, and new explanation.
